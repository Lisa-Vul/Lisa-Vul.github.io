What is Geo-Graphics?
About
Geo-Graphics is a graphic publication designed by Monash Design students enrolled in the unit, digital publishing.
This book combines the work of each students individual interpretation of the various layers of ‘The Stack’.

The Stack is heavily embedded into our everyday life. Our world is becoming reorganised into information. If this information acts as a copy of reality. Like a recording.
Who or what is controlling it?
The user is part of the 'Stack', whereby CAPTCHA also resides. This is explained further in "TELLING HUMANS AND COMPUTERS APART AUTOMATICALLY" which describes CAPTCHA as:
"a test, any test, that can be automatically generated, which most humans can pass, but that current computer programs cannot pass. Notice the paradox, a CAPTCHA is a program that can generate and grade tests that it itself cannot pass"
What is CAPTCHA?
This chapter highlights the challenges users face when completing the 'classic' CAPTCHA tasks. Have you ever thought about CAPTCHA? Who made it? Why does it exist and what structures are needed for it to work consistently and effectively?

As we focus specifically on background noises, anti-recognition, two-level structures and other techniques related to the design world be wary that CAPTCHA can take many forms and is continuously progessing.
