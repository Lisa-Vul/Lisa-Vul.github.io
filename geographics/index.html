What is Geo-Graphics?
About
Geo-Graphics is a graphic publication designed by Monash Design students enrolled in the digital publishing unit. This book combines the work of each student who have individually interpretted various parts of ‘The Stack’.

The Stack is heavily embedded into our everyday life, it is literally impossible to experience our reality without mediation by technology. Our world is becoming reorganised into information. If this information acts as a copy of reality. Like a recording. Who or what is controlling it?
In the words of Erik Davis:
"the onrushing proliferation of digital content channels and interactive media was going to savage “consensus reality” as well. It wasn’t just the technology that was going to change; the mass mind itself was"
What does this particular chapter contain?
Have you ever thought about captcha? Who made it? Why does it exist and what structures are needed for it to work consistently and effectively. This chapter informs the reader of the challenges users face when completing the original captcha tasks.

Captcha can take many forms and is continuously progessing, however, this chapter focuses on specifically on background noises, anti-recognition, two-level structures and other design related techniques.
